The ChatGPT content calendar prompt (plan 30 days in 10 minutes)

Most people ask ChatGPT for "30 post ideas" and get 30 random, disconnected topics. The fix isn't a better idea list — it's giving each day a job. Here's the copy-paste prompt, plus the one framework that makes the calendar actually get you clients.

First: why "give me 30 post ideas" fails

A pile of random topics has no strategy behind it. Some posts should pull in strangers, some should build trust, and a few should actually invite the sale. If every post does the same thing, you either teach into the void or sell to people who don't trust you yet. So before the prompt, here's the framework it uses:

JobWhat the post doesShare
AttractReaches strangers — leads with their problem~40%
NurtureBuilds trust — teaches one useful thing~40%
ConvertInvites the ready ones to take a step~20%

The copy-paste prompt

Paste this into ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini and fill the brackets:

You're my content strategist. Build me a 30-day social media content calendar.

About me: [what you do] for [who you help].
This month's focus: [one theme/offer you want to build toward].

Rules:
- Assign each of the 30 days one job: Attract, Nurture, or Convert.
- Use roughly 40% Attract, 40% Nurture, 20% Convert.
- Attract posts = lead with my audience's problem.
- Nurture posts = teach one specific, useful thing.
- Convert posts = tie to my offer with a soft call-to-action.

Output a table: Day | Job | Post topic (one line) | Hook idea.
No repeats. Keep topics specific to my niche.
Now you have 30 topics, each with a purpose and a hook — in about 10 minutes. From here you just write each post (or have the AI draft it in your voice) and schedule.
